\begin{abstract}
We present the design and verification of a synthesizable RTL accelerator for
running a frontier open-weight mixture-of-experts (MoE) language model---the
753-billion-parameter GLM-5.2, in its published $\sim$467\,GB GGUF $k$-quant
form---as a single-user, fully offline appliance. The work is deliberately
\emph{verification-first} and \emph{pre-silicon}: we do not report a fabricated
chip or a measured end-to-end token rate. Instead we contribute (i) a bit-exact
hardware datapath for GGUF $k$-quant (Q4\_K/Q6\_K/Q8\_0) dequantization and the
assembled model forward pass, cross-validated bitwise against llama.cpp's own
\code{ggml} kernels on 376{,}586{,}240 weights drawn from two real published
GGUF files, which closes the ``self-referential golden'' gap endemic to
reimplementation-based accelerators; (ii) a memory-bandwidth-bound design
methodology for full-residency MoE decoding, in which a roofline parameterized
by \emph{measured} MoE expert-union growth $U(K)$ and multi-token-prediction
(MTP) speculative accept rates is validated on real RTL cycles by a
cycle-accurate emulation that confirms the residency stall mechanism
(a $\sim$73$\times$ reduction in exposed storage stall); and (iii) a
verification-first methodology with an explicit, reproducible ledger that
separates \emph{proven} (bit-exact and formal), \emph{elaborated} (structural),
and \emph{estimated} (roofline) claims, including formal verification of the
memory/control plane and a parameter-equivalence proof for result-invariant
configuration knobs. The compute datapath places and routes on a commodity
Kintex UltraScale+ FPGA, and we derive a retail-parts prototype design point
(twenty 24\,GB LPDDR5X packages on a 1280-bit bus, 480\,GB resident) supported by
an analytical board routability study. Throughout, projected token rates are
tagged as estimates; the sole remaining physical gate---an ASIC tapeout---is
named, not hidden.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

Frontier large language models are increasingly available as open weights, yet
running the largest of them locally---offline, on data that cannot leave a
network---remains out of reach for most users. The obstacle is not compute but
memory bandwidth. A 753B-parameter MoE model quantized to roughly four bits
occupies $\sim$467\,GB; each decoded token reads on the order of 25\,GB of
active weights, so single-stream throughput is governed by
$\text{bandwidth} \div \text{bytes-per-token}$, and arithmetic units spend most
of their time waiting. Consumer multi-GPU rigs cannot hold 467\,GB in aggregate
VRAM, so weights spill to host memory and stream over PCIe; the effective
bandwidth---not the number of GPUs---then caps throughput at a few tokens per
second. Datacenter deployments that keep the whole model resident in
high-bandwidth memory reach hundreds of tokens per second, but at a cost and
form factor incompatible with a single-user, sub-\$5k appliance.

This paper studies the middle of that gap: an application-specific design that
holds the entire model resident in a single, wide, moderately-fast memory pool,
purpose-built to saturate the memory-bound roofline rather than to provide
general-purpose FLOPS. The target is a local box that runs the full model with
the network disconnected---an audit as literal as ``does it still work with the
Ethernet cable unplugged?''---which is precisely the property air-gapped
organizations require and which cloud, in-VPC, and trusted-execution deployments
by construction cannot provide.

A design of this kind is only credible if its claims are checkable. Machine-
learning accelerators are frequently validated against a reimplementation of the
reference numerics, leaving a ``self-referential golden'' gap: the hardware may
be bit-exact to the author's own reference while diverging from the software the
community actually runs. We therefore treat verification as the primary
deliverable and organize every claim into a ledger of three kinds:
\emph{proven} (a gated bit-exact simulation or a solver proof), \emph{elaborated}
(a structural check with no functional golden), and \emph{estimated} (a roofline
model). We are explicit about what this work is \emph{not}: there is no
fabricated silicon, and every token rate is a roofline projection. What is
established on real artifacts is stated as such; what is projected is tagged
\est.

\paragraph{Contributions.}
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.4em,itemsep=2pt]
\item \textbf{A bit-exact GGUF $k$-quant datapath, cross-validated against
llama.cpp.} The RTL dequantization of Q4\_K, Q6\_K, and Q8\_0 super-blocks, and
the assembled GLM-5.2 forward pass, are bit-exact to an independent \code{ggml}
reference; that reference is in turn shown bitwise-identical to llama.cpp's own
\code{dequantize\_row\_*} kernels on 376{,}586{,}240 weights from two real
published GGUF files. By transitivity the hardware dequantization equals the
real file's \code{ggml} dequantization, closing the self-referential gap at the
quantization layer (Section~\ref{sec:verif}).

\item \textbf{A memory-bandwidth-bound residency design methodology with measured
inputs.} We parameterize a decode-time roofline by the MoE expert-union growth
$U(K)$ under speculative decoding and by the MTP accept-rate $r$, both measured
on a same-family model, and we validate the underlying stall mechanism on real
RTL cycles with a cycle-accurate emulation (Sections~\ref{sec:design}
and~\ref{sec:analysis}).

\item \textbf{A verification-first methodology and reproducible ledger.} Formal
model checking (BMC and unbounded $k$-induction) of the memory/control plane, a
sequential-equivalence proof that result-invariant configuration parameters do
not change the default netlist, and an explicit proven/elaborated/estimated
accounting that is reproducible from the repository (Section~\ref{sec:verif}).

\item \textbf{A retail-parts prototype design point with an analytical
routability study.} A residency configuration built from commodity 24\,GB
LPDDR5X packages on a 1280-bit board-level bus, with a first-principles escape-
routing and placement analysis of a 12-layer HDI board (Section~\ref{sec:analysis}).
\end{enumerate}

\section{Background}
\label{sec:bg}

\paragraph{GGUF $k$-quants.} \code{llama.cpp} and its \code{ggml} tensor library~\cite{llamacpp}
are the de-facto substrate for local inference, and GGUF is their model format.
The $k$-quant family stores weights in 256-element super-blocks with a compact
hierarchy of scales: Q4\_K packs 256 four-bit weights with six-bit sub-block
scales and minima into 144 bytes; Q6\_K uses 210 bytes; Q8\_0 stores 32-element
blocks of eight-bit weights with an FP16 scale. A ``dynamic'' mix such as
UD-Q4\_K\_XL keeps most tensors at Q4\_K while promoting sensitive tensors to
higher precision. Matching this format exactly---not a nearby integer
quantization---is what lets an accelerator consume the same file the community
already trusts.

\paragraph{Fine-grained MoE and MLA.} GLM-5.2~\cite{glmweights} is a fine-grained
MoE model~\cite{moe}: each token routes to a small top-$k$ subset of many experts,
so the \emph{set} of weights touched per token is data-dependent. It also uses
multi-head latent attention (MLA)~\cite{deepseekv2}, which compresses the
key/value cache, and a multi-token-prediction (MTP) head usable as a
self-speculative draft in the sense of speculative decoding~\cite{specdec}.

\paragraph{Memory-bound roofline.} For single-stream decoding of a model whose
active weights exceed cache, the classic roofline model~\cite{roofline} reduces
to a bandwidth bound: throughput is
$\text{\tps} = \text{BW} \cdot A \,/\, B_{\text{tok}}$,
where BW is sustained weight bandwidth, $B_{\text{tok}}$ is bytes read per
committed token, and $A$ is the mean number of committed tokens per model pass
(the speculative amplification). The design problem is to make BW large and
$B_{\text{tok}}/A$ small; the compute must merely keep up.

\section{Accelerator Design}
\label{sec:design}

\paragraph{Bit-exact datapath.} The core is a Q4\_K general matrix-multiply
(GEMM) unit that dequantizes super-blocks to FP32 and accumulates in a
documented fixed order, followed by conversion to bf16. Mixed-type columns
(Q6\_K, Q8\_0, F16) are handled by per-column type routing in the same GEMM,
fed by a weight loader that carries a per-tile type descriptor. The surrounding
operators---MLA attention with data-dependent sparse (DSA) key selection, the
SwiGLU expert feed-forward, the sigmoid-gated top-$k$ MoE router, RMSNorm,
interleaved RoPE, and softmax---are assembled into a full \code{glm\_model\_q4k}
forward pass. The entire compute datapath is bit-exact to an assembled numpy
reference that imports the same $k$-quant dequantization used for the
cross-check of Section~\ref{sec:verif}.

\paragraph{Memory system.} A single top-level module wraps the compute die with
a tier-agnostic banked read crossbar, a routed-expert cache with prefetch, an
MLA latent-KV pager with NVMe overflow, weight and boot loaders, and multi-clock
domain-crossing FIFOs. The same RTL serves three configurations selected by
parameters: a streaming demonstrator (weights streamed from storage), a
full-residency SKU (the whole checkpoint resident in DRAM, expert refills served
by the DRAM-tier crossbar), and a hybrid. Residency is expressed by a
\code{RESIDENT} parameter; with it set, expert refills complete as tagged
crossbar reads and the storage path carries only KV spill and the one-time boot
copy, an invariant checked in simulation.

\paragraph{Adaptive speculative chain.} Self-speculative MTP decoding proposes a
chain of draft tokens that the model verifies in a single pass. Because
verification preserves the greedy result exactly, the speculative loop is
\emph{output-invariant} for any draft-depth schedule---it changes throughput,
never the emitted tokens. We exploit this with a runtime-adaptive draft depth
that observes the recent accept rate and adjusts $K$, settling at the
memory-bound optimum without any change to results.

\paragraph{Result-invariant knobs.} Several parameters (a compute-resource knob,
the residency mode, the adaptive-depth mode) are designed to be
\emph{result-invariant}: enabling them must not change the emitted tokens. We
verify this both functionally (bit-exact test gates with the knob on and off)
and structurally (a sequential-equivalence proof that the default-parameter
netlist is unchanged), so the knobs are optimizations, not risks.

\section{Verification Methodology}
\label{sec:verif}

Verification is organized as a ledger. Each claim is \emph{proven} (a gated
functional/bit-exact simulation or a solver proof), \emph{elaborated} (a
structural elaboration with no functional golden), or \emph{estimated} (a
roofline model). Table~\ref{tab:ledger} summarizes the state; the remainder of
this section describes the load-bearing pieces.

\paragraph{Closing the self-referential gap.} The assembled forward pass is
bit-exact to a numpy golden, but that golden is the author's own reimplementation
of \code{ggml}. To close the gap to the software the community runs, we parse
two real published GGUF files, extract every Q4\_K, Q6\_K, and Q8\_0 tensor's raw
super-blocks, and dequantize each block two ways: with our reference, and with
llama.cpp's own \code{dequantize\_row\_q4\_K}/\code{\_q6\_K}/\code{\_q8\_0}
compiled against the built \code{ggml} library. The FP32 outputs are compared
bitwise (as \code{uint32}, not within a tolerance). All 376{,}586{,}240 weights
match; Q8\_0 is cross-confirmed across two distributions. By transitivity with
the existing RTL$\,\equiv\,$reference gates, the hardware dequantization equals
the real file's \code{ggml} dequantization. We are careful to scope the claim:
whole-runtime numeric equality with llama.cpp (attention and accumulation
orders) is \emph{out of contract} by design; our contract is \code{ggml}-exact
dequantization plus a documented FP32 accumulation order.

\paragraph{Formal verification of the control plane.} The memory-system
controllers---the banked crossbar, the storage-read fabric, the boot loader, the
KV pager, the speculative-decode sequencer, and the expert cache in both
prefetch-off and prefetch-on modes---are checked by bounded model checking and,
where tractable, unbounded $k$-induction, with no counterexample from reset.
Documented residual bounded gaps are recorded rather than hidden.

\paragraph{Equivalence of result-invariant knobs.} For the residency mode we
prove, by sequential equivalence checking (combinational and inductive), that
the default-parameter top-level module is logically identical to the pre-feature
commit; the feature therefore adds behavior only when explicitly enabled and
cannot perturb the verified default. The same discipline (a formal or structural
identity proof, not a promise) is applied to the other result-invariant knobs.

\section{Design-Space Analysis and Evaluation}
\label{sec:analysis}

\paragraph{Measured roofline inputs.} The bytes-per-token denominator has two
parts that speculation amortizes differently. The routed-expert stream is
$\approx$14\,GB/token at $K{=}1$, and under a speculative chain the \emph{union}
of experts touched across the verified draft positions grows by a factor $U(K)$;
the resident hot set (attention projections, dense and shared FFN, router, LM
head) is $\approx$11\,GB/token, read once per pass. We measure $U(K)$ directly
by tracing exact expert selections on a same-family model (GLM-4.5-Air):
$U(2){\approx}1.6$, $U(4){\approx}2.65$, $U(6){\approx}3.5$, $U(8){\approx}4.3$.
We also measure the MTP accept rate---first-position $r_1{\approx}0.87$ with a
steep per-position decay---which, combined with $U(K)$, places the memory-bound
optimum at a shallow depth ($K{=}1$--2) and an effective amplification
$A_{\text{eff}}$ plateau near 2.9. The adaptive controller settles at this
optimum by construction.

\paragraph{Cycle-accurate validation of the mechanism.} A cycle-accurate
testbench drives the assembled system with a storage read-latency model engaged,
holding every committed token bit-exact against a standalone reference while
counting cycles. On the verified slice the compute-die baseline is
$\approx$10{,}896 cycles/token. Exposed storage stall is linear in read latency
and proportional to miss count---the memory-bound assumption, now \emph{measured}
rather than assumed---and the residency configuration makes stall independent of
storage latency: at a 1024-cycle latency, streaming exposes 2{,}567 stall
cycles/token whereas residency exposes 35, a $\sim$73$\times$ reduction. This
validates the residency pivot on real cycles even though the absolute product
token rate remains a projection.

\paragraph{FPGA fit.} The Q4\_K product top, in a compact configuration, places
and routes on a Kintex UltraScale+ XCKU3P: 87.5\% LUT utilization, hold met, and
a routed maximum frequency of 46.5\,MHz reached through a sequence of
bit-exactness-preserving latency-only repipelining rounds. This establishes that
the datapath is physically realizable on commodity silicon; it is a compact
slice, not the full model at product speed, and we do not extrapolate a token
rate from it.

\paragraph{Projected throughput.} Combining the measured bandwidth of a resident
LPDDR5X pool with the measured $U(K)$ and $r$ inputs, the roofline projects a
residency design point of order $80$--$110$\,\tps\est\ for the single-user box
--- presuming a MAC array sized to consume that bandwidth ($\sim$4.7--5.2K dequant
lanes at 490\,MHz for the 1.1\,TB/s point, $\sim$6.6--7.3K for 1.54\,TB/s, the spread
being an unresolved bits-per-weight inconsistency in our own spec); with a smaller
array it is the array, not the memory, that binds, and the roofline is not
reached ---
against $\sim$7\,\tps\ observed on a four-GPU offloading rig (bandwidth-starved)
and $\sim$15--25\,\tps\ reported for a same-class 512\,GB unified-memory
workstation. These are projections with measured inputs, not silicon
measurements.

\paragraph{Prototype design point and routability.} A physically buildable
prototype uses commodity retail parts: twenty 24\,GB LPDDR5X packages on a
1280-bit board-level bus give 480\,GB resident at $\approx$1.54\,TB/s. Because
the wide bus leaves the package, we perform a first-principles routability study.
An escape-routing model (via-in-pad HDI, the ring-cut channel-crossing bound)
estimates that the $\sim$2{,}800-signal escape of the host BGA needs on the order
of four signal layers, within the six a 12-layer stack (6 signal / 4 ground /
2 power) provides; a placement check confirms the twenty packages and the host
fit within a 130$\times$110\,mm board with courtyard and edge clearance. This is
analysis, not a routed board: the layer count is an estimate with roughly
$\pm$1--2 layers of uncertainty, and a full place-and-route confirms the final
margin.

\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

Offloading systems (e.g.\ weight streaming and paged attention in software
stacks~\cite{llamacpp}) make large models runnable on limited memory but remain
bounded by the slowest tier's bandwidth, which is precisely the wall this design
removes by full residency. Wafer-scale and SRAM-resident accelerators reach very
high token rates by holding weights in on-chip SRAM, but at a capacity-per-die
that makes a
467\,GB single-box deployment economically impossible; they occupy a different
bracket. Unified-memory workstations approximate the residency idea with a wide
LPDDR pool and are the closest existing comparison, at roughly half the bandwidth
of the prototype design point studied here. Relative to all of these, our
contribution is not a new attention kernel or a new quantization, but a
verification-first hardware realization of an existing, community-trusted
quantization format together with a measured-input design methodology.

\section{Limitations and Honest Scope}
\label{sec:limits}

We restate the boundaries plainly. (1)~There is no fabricated silicon; the sole
remaining physical gate is an ASIC tapeout (an LPDDR5X PHY IP license plus
mask/NRE cost), which no amount of RTL verification removes. (2)~All token rates
are roofline projections tagged \est; the FPGA result is a compact
place-and-route, not the full model at speed, and the cycle-accurate numbers are
on a verified slice. (3)~Our numeric contract is \code{ggml}-exact
dequantization plus a documented accumulation order; whole-runtime equality with
llama.cpp is deliberately out of contract. (4)~The board routability result is
an analytical estimate with a dummy host ball-out, not a routed, signal-integrity-
closed design. (5)~Deployment, cost, and business claims are out of scope for
this paper. We regard stating these boundaries as part of the contribution: for a
system an auditor must sign off on, an honest ledger is the argument, not a
blanket claim.

\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:concl}

We have described a verification-first RTL accelerator for local GGUF $k$-quant
LLM inference. The datapath is bit-exact to the community's own software on real
model bytes; the memory-bandwidth-bound residency design is parameterized by
measured MoE and speculative-decoding inputs and validated on real RTL cycles;
and the control plane is formally verified, with result-invariant configuration
knobs proven not to disturb the default netlist. The compute datapath is
physically realizable on a commodity FPGA, and a retail-parts prototype design
point is supported by an analytical routability study. Nothing physically
impossible appears in the path from here to a device; what remains is a tapeout,
which we name rather than hide. The full engineering record, and every gate that
backs the claims above, is open.
