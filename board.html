AIPU appliance board

A concept design point for the rung-③ silicon appliance: a 512 GB LPDDR5X full-residency box holding the whole ~467 GB Q4_K checkpoint in DRAM. No board has been built. Every throughput, power, and cost figure below is an estimate [EST]; the physical PHYs are vendor IP, TB-stubbed in RTL.

The measured, in-repo result is the FPGA fit (rung ①) shown directly below. The board on this page is the rung-③ endpoint of the hardware ladder — a modeled target, not fabricated hardware.

02

Rung-③ primary design point

A 512 GB LPDDR5X full-residency box. The whole ~467 GB Q4_K checkpoint is DRAM-resident, so expert-reuse h = 1 by construction — there is no streaming tier. Cold storage is one M.2 NVMe; boot-load is ~70 s.

Memory
16 × 32 GB LPDDR5X · 1024-bit on-package / on-substrate bus · ~1.1 TB/s
Full residency of the ~467 GB checkpoint in 512 GB of DRAM.
Cold storage
One M.2 NVMe · boot-load ~70 s
Load-once at boot; no per-token streaming path.
Throughput
≈80 tok/s [measured-inputs EST]
Adaptive spec-chain; U(K) and the MTP accept rate r are GLM-family measured. ~95 if GLM-5.2's deeper MTP hits its published accept depth. A ~1.54 TB/s "v3-proto" variant models ~111 [EST].
Power
≥50–78 W [EST, re-derived]
DRAM rail 35–53 W from 4–6 pJ/bit. The SoC term is UNVERIFIED — the older ~40–60 W figure rested on a rail that did not reproduce, so this is a re-derived estimate.
SoC / compute
12–16 nm · ~9.4K MAC lanes @ 490 MHz
Sized to consume 1.1 TB/s (~12.7K MAC lanes for the 1.54 TB/s v3-proto variant).
Physical / BOM
Board 120 × 80 mm · ~$1.8–2.4k [EST]
On-substrate 1024-bit memory. BOM is a planning number, not a quote — memory/storage/board-dominated; the compute die is a minority of cost.
